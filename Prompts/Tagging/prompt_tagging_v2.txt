Definitions of National Origin, Ethnicity, and Race:
1. National Origin: The national origin of a speaker refers to the nationality associated with the nation where the speaker was born or where their ancestors originated from. National origin is not the name of a nation but its nationality. Examples of national origins include Chinese, Korean, Vietnamese, Cambodian, Thai, Iranian, Filipino, Mexican, Dominican, Cuban, Puerto Rican, Spanish, Portuguese, etc.

2. Race: A speaker's race can be categorized into one of seven groups: White, Black or African American, Asian, American Indian or Alaska Native, Native Hawaiian or Other Pacific Islander, Multiracial, and Unknown.

3. Ethnicity: Ethnicity denotes the Hispanic origin of the speaker and can fall into one of the following three groups: Hispanic or Latinx, Non-Hispanic or Non-Latinx, and Unknown.

Types of Identification:
A line can have three distinct types of identification:

1. Self-Identification: This type involves a speaker explicitly identifying, implying, or directly specifying their own national origin, ethnicity, or race through their own words.

2. Other-Person In-Interaction Identification: In this category, a speaker identifies, implies, or directly specifies the ethnicity, national origin, or race of another speaker participating in the conversation. This is often done using second-person or third-person pronouns, but there can be exceptions.

3. Other-Person Outside-Interaction Identification: This form of identification occurs when a speaker identifies, implies, or directly specifies the ethnicity, national origin, or race of an individual who is not part of the ongoing conversation. This is often done using third-person pronouns, but there can be exceptions.

Requirements for Self-Identification and Other-Person In-Interaction Identification:
For both self-identification and other-person in-interaction identification, it is crucial that both the person identifying and the person being identified (whose race, national origin, or ethnicity is being specified) are active speakers within the ongoing conversation.
